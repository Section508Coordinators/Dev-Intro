Overview

Welcome to the DHS OAST Section 508 Playbook series support site on the Section508Coordinators GitHub!

This Section508Coordinators GitHub site has many useful areas and assets. To help orient those interested in the support available for the Section 508 Playbook series, DHS OAST has provided this series of GitHub navigational pages to serve as a roadmap to the technical support assets available to you on GitHub in support of the playbooks.

What is the Playbook Series?

As the name implies, the Section 508 Playbook Series is a collection of legal, business, and technical best practice strategies, aka "plays". These plays are intended to provide DHS Component staff with readily available, go-to strategies for proactively addressing the many challenging activities associated with delivering the ICT services of software development, testing, and procurement as they apply to conformance with Section 508 Law.

What's new about the Playbooks?

Plenty! The main thrust of the playbook series is to provide modern and relevant guidance for addressing the issues that arise from a constantly-changing world It takes into account the new Section 508 revised guidelines, the new ICT Testing Baseline for Web, current industry trends in software development to induce modern UI Frameworks, test automation & Dev-Ops, universal design expectations and enhanced monitoring and collaboration techniques within technical teams such as agile principles.

This GitHub site provides a location for examples, guideline documentation, and other resources to help get the most out of the playbook series and your team's efforts.

DHS OAST wants you!

In the spirit of teamwork, DHS OAST wants your participation in the following activities to enhance the effectiveness and relevance of the Playbook series:

Playbook series content feedback

OAST wants you to provide thoughtful feedback on the Playbook series content. Tell us what works, what doesn't, and what we should improve upon. Reach out to the accessibility help desk to get access to the Playbook content and set up a convenient feedback loop for providing your valuable input.

Playbook pilot program

OAST wants you to participate in piloting some of the paradigm shifts, guidance, and best practices that are recommended in the Playbooks. OAST will reach out shortly to connect with technical and acquisition teams willing to help vet the effectiveness of some of the "plays" by putting Playbook concepts into action on a small scale.

On the horizon...

The following OAST activities are currently in the works and should be available shortly:

  • Communities of practice (CoP)
    • Trusted Tester: OAST is in the process of providing an enhanced platform as well as gearing up for the ability to support issues surrounding the new Trusted Tester version 5 for Web training and test methodology questions and issues

    • Developers and test automation engineers: OAST is expanding its CoPs by adding developer and test automation CoPs to support the guidance and best practices promoted within the new Developer and Tester playbooks respectively.
